## Answer: The Opportunities Residence Act is a German law that allows some individuals with a tolerated status to obtain a permanent right of residence more quickly.
## Numbers: 1, 2

## Answer: The requirements for this new right of residence include possession of a tolerated stay permit, no criminal offenses, no false statements or deception about identity, and commitment to the free democratic basic order.
## Numbers: 3-7

## Answer: Those affected receive a residence permit for 18 months and during this time, they are given the opportunity to fulfill the requirements for a permanent right of residence in Germany, which includes being able to secure their livelihood mainly through gainful employment, having proof of identity, and being able to demonstrate a good command of German.
