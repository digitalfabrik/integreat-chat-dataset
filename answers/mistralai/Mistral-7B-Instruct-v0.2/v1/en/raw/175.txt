## Answer: The residence documents include proof of arrival for asylum seekers, residence permits for asylum seekers and recognized refugees, a fictitious certificate for recognized refugees, and an acquiescence for tolerated individuals.
## Numbers: 1, 2, 3, 5

## Answer: Residence permits for asylum seekers and recognized refugees provide information about the status in the asylum procedure, any restrictions on gainful employment, and the period of validity.
## Numbers: 11, 14, 17, 24, 27

## Answer: Proof of arrival documents are issued to persons who have applied for asylum and are valid for the period between registration as an asylum seeker and the official application for asylum at the Federal Office for Migration and Refugees (BAMF).
## Numbers: 6, 9

## Answer: Residence permits for recognized refugees are issued after a positive decision on the asylum application and open up the possibility of a later permanent right of residence (settlement permit).
## Numbers: 25, 30

## Answer: A tolerated stay is a suspension of deportation issued temporarily if deportation is impossible for legal, factual, humanitarian or personal reasons.
## Numbers: 35, 36, 38, 39

## Answer: The responsible authorities for job placement and social benefits vary depending on the status of the individual. For asylum seekers, the Employment Agency and Social welfare office are responsible. For recognized refugees and tolerated individuals, the Jobcenter and Social welfare office are responsible.
