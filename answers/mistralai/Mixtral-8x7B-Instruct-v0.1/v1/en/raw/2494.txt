## Answer: The Opportunities Residence Act is a new law in Germany that allows those who have been living in the country for many years with a tolerated status to obtain a permanent right of residence more quickly. The requirements for this new right of residence include possession of a tolerated stay permit, no criminal offenses, no false statements or deception about identity, and commitment to the free democratic basic order. The residence permit is issued for 18 months and cannot be extended.
